# PART VII: Q&A PREPARATION — 30 HARDEST QUESTIONS

**Q1: "Your short on GEV — aren't you fighting the strongest company in the sector?"**
A: We're not calling GEV a bad business. We're saying at 45x PE, the market prices in perfection and zero Chinese competition. The pair trade captures relative value shift. Even if GEV goes up, DEC should go up more as export optionality gets priced in. We'd note that 30/30 covering analysts are bullish — peak consensus historically correlates with peak relative performance.

**Q2: "The Canadian order is only 20 units. How is that material?"**
A: At Citi's CNY 200M unit price, that's CNY 4B in equipment revenue with 40-50% gross margin — potentially CNY 1.6-2.0B in gross profit. For a company with CNY 3.5B baseline net income, that's transformative. But more importantly, it VALIDATES the thesis. The value isn't just the Canadian order — it's the signal that a multi-year export cycle has begun. Stock re-ratings happen on confirmation of inflection, not on peak revenue.

**Q3: "What happens if tariffs specifically target gas turbines?"**
A: Three responses. First, the Canadian order was signed in March 2026, during the most protectionist trade environment in decades — it's already happening DESPITE tariffs. Second, gas turbines are physics-based equipment (no IP theft concern), which makes them politically easier to exempt than semiconductors or software. Third, even if US-specific tariffs block entry, DEC can pivot to Middle East, SE Asia, Central Asia, and Latin America — the IEA projects 200+ GW new gas capacity globally by 2030.

**Q4: "Why not Sieyuan Electric instead of Dongfang?"**
A: Sieyuan at 57x PE and all-time highs already prices in the grid CapEx story. It has zero gas turbine export exposure. DEC gives you grid CapEx PLUS export optionality — and the export piece is priced at zero in the current market cap. Our SOTP analysis shows that stripping out the export segment, DEC's domestic business alone justifies the current price. You're getting the export call option for free.

**Q5: "Your AI module — did you actually build this or is it conceptual?"**
A: We built it. [Show the code, the Claude API call logs, the raw transcript outputs.] The tool processed 8 GE Vernova earnings call transcripts and identified a 450% increase in China/competition mentions from Q1 2024 to Q4 2025. **Q6: "Chinese SOEs typically trade at a discount. Why would DEC re-rate?"**
A: The SOE discount is appropriate for the domestic business and it's already baked into the current valuation. But the export revenue is earned in competitive international markets — it should be valued without the SOE discount. Additionally, SOE reform under the 15th Five-Year Plan is pushing profitability metrics and dividend increases, which could narrow the discount over time.

**Q7: "What's your timeframe? Gas turbine displacement takes years."**
A: Stocks re-rate on expectations, not delivery. The catalyst isn't revenue recognition from the Canadian order (2027-2029). It's the SECOND and THIRD export orders that validate the multi-year thesis. If DEC announces another developed-market order in Q2-Q3 2026, the market will begin discounting a multi-year export revenue stream. This is exactly what happened with Chinese solar companies at their export inflection points.

**Q8: "How do you hedge the currency risk?"**
A: The pair trade has a natural hedge. If CNY appreciates (likely with China's export surge), the long benefits in HKD terms. If CNY depreciates, DEC's export competitiveness improves (cheaper in USD terms), supporting the underlying business thesis. Either direction supports the trade.

The 13% CAGR is what the MARKET implies at $832/share — we're not arguing it's right or wrong, we're showing what you need to BELIEVE to justify the current price.
